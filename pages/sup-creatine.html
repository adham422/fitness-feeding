Are there any functions for creatine in the body?

1- Increase the size of cells by providing them with the necessary water and moisture. 2- Increase endurance while exercising. 3- Reducing myostatin levels in the body, which may slow down and reduce muscle growth and hypertrophy. 4- Improving signals and connections between cells which increases the effectiveness of muscles in repairing any damage. 5- Reducing the breakdown of proteins, which increases muscle mass in the body.

Benefits of creatine

1- Fighting Parkinson's disease Parkinson's disease occurs when the level of the main neurotransmitter in the brain (dopamine) decreases, and it has been found that creatine has a role in raising dopamine levels in the brain and preventing their decline. Therefore, having Parkinson's patients gradually engage in weight lifting exercises may help fight their Parkinson's disease 2- Fight diabetes Taking creatine supplements may help lower blood sugar levels, especially when taken with regular exercise. 3- Muscle and cell growth It helps stimulate the production of special molecules that help cells burn calories and raise energy consumption levels the body especially during exercise.

Other benefits of creatine for the body

Creatine has many other benefits as well, including the following: 1- Improve brain functions - Increasing the ability to concentrate 3- Alleviating symptoms of depression. 4- Increase bone density and mass.

Recommended doses of it

-It is preferable to start by taking 4 doses of it daily over a period of 5-7 days, and the amount of each dose of each dose is 5 grams in what is called the storage phase. -Its absorption into the muscles usually improves with eating foods rich in proteins or carbohydrates, after which only 3-5 grams of creatine are taken daily.
